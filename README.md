# High-Temperature-Alumina-Ceramic-Quench-Disc-Assembly

Designed two Alumina ceramic discs for experiments involving high-temperature flames. Single-port and ten-port models were designed. 
Alumina was chosen for it's stability within higher-temperature enviornments. The 0.010" diameter ports shouldn't enlarge when exposed to higher temperatures. 
However, in addition to high-temperature tolerance, pressure through the small orifice, increases the velocity of the combustible gases, extendind the flame front from the surface of the disc. This means that during testing, the surface of the disc is within touching temperatures.
